Chapter 2

The Sixteenth Birthday

Aurora returned to the cottage, her heart alight with excitement. She shared her encounter with the fairies, who immediately recognized the danger. It was the eve of her sixteenth birthday, and they knew Maleficent would stop at nothing to fulfill her curse. Despite their fears, the fairies decided to reveal Aurora's true identity and take her back to the palace. They hoped that within the castle walls, she would be safe from Maleficent's reach.

The Tragic Encounter

Aurora was heartbroken to leave the forest and her newfound love behind, but she obeyed the fairies. Upon arriving at the palace, she was introduced to her parents. The reunion was joyous but tinged with fear for the impending curse. That evening, as the castle bustled with preparations for her birthday celebration, Aurora wandered through the halls. Lured by an eerie, otherworldly music, she found herself in an abandoned tower where an old spinning wheel stood. Enchanted, she reached out and pricked her finger. Instantly, she collapsed into a deep sleep.

The Sleeping Kingdom

The curse did not stop with Aurora. Maleficent, in her malevolent glee, cast a spell over the entire kingdom, causing everyone to fall into a deep sleep. Thorns and brambles grew around the castle, creating an impenetrable barrier. Prince Phillip, upon hearing of Aurora's true identity and the curse, rode to the castle. When he arrived, he found it surrounded by thick, enchanted thorns. Armed with a magical sword and shield given to him by the fairies, he fought his way through the thicket.
