Chapter 1

The Curse

Once upon a time, in a kingdom surrounded by verdant hills and crystal-clear rivers, a joyful occasion was taking place. King Frederick and Queen Eleanor had just welcomed their long-awaited daughter, Princess Aurora. The entire kingdom celebrated, and a grand feast was held in her honor. To bestow blessings upon the newborn princess, seven fairies from distant lands were invited. Each fairy brought a special gift: beauty, grace, wisdom, kindness, musical talent, and a pure heart. Just as the seventh fairy was about to present her gift, the doors of the grand hall burst open. In swept Maleficent, an ancient and powerful sorceress who had not been invited. Offended by the slight, Maleficent approached the cradle and pronounced a terrible curse. "On her sixteenth birthday, Princess Aurora will prick her finger on a spindle and fall into a deep sleep from which she shall never awaken." Gasps filled the hall. However, the seventh fairy had not yet given her blessing. "Though I cannot undo the curse," she said, "I can soften it. Princess Aurora will indeed fall into a deep sleep, but she will awaken with true love's kiss."

Hidden Away

To protect their beloved daughter, King Frederick and Queen Eleanor decreed that all spinning wheels in the kingdom be destroyed. Despite this effort, they were still fearful. So, they entrusted Aurora to the care of three good-hearted, wise fairies: Flora, Fauna, and Merryweather. In a secluded cottage deep in the forest, the fairies raised Aurora as their own, under the guise of an ordinary peasant girl named Briar Rose. Aurora grew up blissfully unaware of her royal heritage, loved and cherished by her fairy guardians. Years passed, and Aurora blossomed into a beautiful young woman with golden hair and a voice as sweet as a nightingale. The fairies ensured she never encountered a spindle, and they kept her hidden from Maleficent's searching eyes.

The Prince

In a neighboring kingdom, Prince Phillip grew up with stories of the beautiful Princess Aurora. Though he had never met her, the tales of her grace and kindness captured his imagination. He often wandered through the forests, dreaming of a day when he might meet his fabled princess. One sunny afternoon, while riding through the forest, Phillip heard a melodious voice singing a hauntingly beautiful song. Drawn to the source, he found Aurora, dancing and singing with the woodland creatures. Their eyes met, and an instant connection formed. Neither revealed their true identities, but they spent the afternoon together, laughing and talking as if they had known each other for years. As the sun began to set, Aurora bid him farewell, promising to meet him again the next day.
